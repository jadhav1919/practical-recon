# Video Search Engines

## 1. Overview

**Video Search Engines** are platforms used to search and discover video content on the internet.

Unlike normal web search engines that mainly index webpages, video search engines focus on:

- videos
- live streams
- recorded presentations
- interviews
- webinars
- tutorials
- public event recordings

Common video search platforms include:

- YouTube
- Google Video Search
- Vimeo
- Dailymotion

In cybersecurity and OSINT, video search engines help gather visual and spoken information from publicly available videos.

![Video Search Overview](images/video-search/video-search-overview.png)

---

## 2. Why It Matters

Videos often reveal much more information than text.

A single public video may accidentally expose:

- office locations
- employee identities
- ID badges
- computer screens
- company infrastructure
- networking devices
- event locations
- internal tools
- security systems

This makes video search useful in:

- OSINT
- Footprinting
- Reconnaissance
- Social engineering research
- Brand investigation

---

## 3. How Video Search Engines Work

Video search engines index video content uploaded to platforms.

They collect information such as:

- video title
- description
- tags
- captions
- spoken keywords
- thumbnails
- upload date
- channel information

When you search, the engine matches your keywords with indexed video metadata and sometimes speech/caption content.

## 11. Risks / Misuse

Attackers may misuse videos to gather:

- employee details
- infrastructure information
- internal screenshots
- office layouts
- technology details

This is why organizations should review what information appears in public videos.

---

## 12. Ethical Note

Use video search engines only for:

- education
- authorized research
- defensive security
- OSINT
- exposure review

Do not use them for:

- stalking
- harassment
- privacy abuse
- unauthorized surveillance

## 13. Key Takeaways

- Video search engines help discover public video content
- Videos can reveal valuable visual and spoken information
- Useful in OSINT and reconnaissance
- Helps identify technology, employees, and infrastructure exposure
- Public videos should be reviewed carefully for information leakage
